10 most communly used
Dark Patterns

1

False Urgency

Deceptive tactics that create a sense of urgency to rush users into making decisions.

2

Basket Sneaking

Adding extra items to a user's shopping cart without their clear consent or knowledge.

3

Confirm Shaming

Using guilt or manipulation in confirmation prompts to discourage users from opting out.

4

Forced Action

Requiring users to take an action they didn't intend or forcing them into unwanted choices.

5

Subscription Trap

Tricking users into unwittingly subscribing to ongoing services with unclear terms.

6

Interface Interference

Design elements deliberately confusing or hindering user navigation for hidden motives.

7

Bait and Switch

Promoting a product or offer and then substituting it with a less desirable alternative.

8

Drop Pricing

Initially lowering prices but later raising them once users are committed or interested.

9

Disguised Advertising

Presenting promotional content in a way that appears as regular, non-promotional information.

10

Nagging

Persistent and annoying reminders or prompts designed to pressure users into specific actions.
